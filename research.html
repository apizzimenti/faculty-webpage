I'm interested in algebra, graph theory, topology, Markov chains, algorithms, civics, and all the things in between. While at the University of Iowa, I worked with the Optimization, Machine Learning, and High-Performance Computing Group to explore the parallelization of graph algorithms, their application to extremely large datasets, and their use in studying the math of social choice: electoral systems, voting theory, redistricting, and the like.

I also work as data scientist and contributor at the MGGG Redistricting Lab, a nonpartisan research organization supported by the Tisch College of Civic Life at Tufts University. The Lab does research, writes software, and works with organizations to explore how math can be used in the name of civil rights. Over the past half-decade, we have curated a significant body of work, including academic research, technical reports, court testimony, and flagship sofware like GerryChain, Districtr, and GerryTools.
